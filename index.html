Investigation & Research

The "Why"
Behind Our
Solution.

Community Need

Stakeholders: Companies that use hourly workers instead of salaried employees face ongoing challenges with workforce management and scheduling efficiency.

Root Causes

Scheduling is a long and tedious problem that can be solved by a program. In theory, automation can eliminate hours of manual work that managers currently face each week.

Evidence from the Field

The manager at Donatos Pizza shared that having people leave without notification can lead to extra undue strain on the business as a whole, making scheduling even more critical.

Innovation
& Action.

From concept to code—how we built a web-based tool that makes adaptable schedules at the customer's request and on demand.

🎯
The Solution

A web-based tool that creates adaptable schedules for customers at their request and on demand, removing the burden from management.

🔄
Iterative Development

Started with a base program that could print work hours, then refined it through multiple iterations based on real-world constraints.

⚖️
Minor Hour Compliance

Encountered problems where minors would be scheduled more hours than necessary or allowed. Fixed by checking output against legal requirements.

📊
Demand-Based Scheduling

Core functionality: adjusts scheduled hours based on availability and demand patterns (e.g., Friday being busier than Monday).

👥
Age Differentiation

Built-in ability to differentiate between minors and adults for ease of use and legal compliance in scheduling.

🛠️
Community Feedback Loop

Refined features based on peer feedback and testing, leading to specific code improvements and feature pivots throughout development.

Impact & Reflection

The "So What?"

Beyond the code lies the real impact: how our solution serves the community and what we learned about technology's role in society.

Personal Growth Reflection

"This project made me realize that coding and roles as technologists in society can be more than just someone sitting in an office. It requires a lot of problem-solving skills that you wouldn't get from a normal application of skills. The real-world constraints and stakeholder needs challenged us in unexpected ways."

📈

Social Impact

Our solution removes the arduous task of scheduling from the duties of management in the service industry. Impact measured by increased efficiency and reduced time burden on managers.

💡

Service Value

Provides tangible service by increasing payment going to the company and boosting workplace efficiency, allowing managers to focus on other critical business tasks.

🚀

Future Scalability

With another semester, we would implement weekly scheduling instead of daily for manager convenience, and refine the logic to provide more realistic shift assignments.

Key
Takeaways.

01

Real-World Problem Solving

Working with actual stakeholders taught us that theory and practice are very different. Constraints like labor laws and variable demand patterns added complexity we hadn't anticipated.

02

Iterative Design Works

Starting simple and building up iteratively was key to our success. Each problem we encountered—from minor hour violations to unrealistic schedules—was solved through refinement, not perfection on the first try.

03

Technology Serves People

The most important lesson: technology is a tool for service. Our code only matters because it helps real people solve real problems. That human element makes all the difference.
